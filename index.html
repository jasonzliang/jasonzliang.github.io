About Me

I am an AI Research Scientist & Principal Architect dedicated to achieving Artificial General Intelligence through the lens of nature-inspired computation and scalable reasoning. My work sits at the intersection of evolutionary optimization, complexity science, and inference-time compute, moving beyond static scaling laws toward open-ended learning and recursive self-improvement.

I build agentic systems for deliberative System-2 reasoning and scale neuroevolutionary paradigms for massive distributed optimization. I architected Caesar, an autonomous deep research agent that uses adversarial self-refinement and generative merging to achieve state-of-the-art creative synthesis. As the inventor of the CoDeepNEAT algorithm, I am eager to apply experience in evolutionary, population-based optimization and architecture search to improve the next generation of AI agents and reasoning models.

I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from UT Austin (2018) under Risto Miikkulainen, and my B.S. in EECS from UC Berkeley (2013). Native Mandarin Chinese speaker; full professional English.
